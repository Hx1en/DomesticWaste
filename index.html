What is Domestic Waste?

Domestic waste, otherwise known as household waste, is any sort of rubbish that is generated as a result of normal day-to-day life within a residential household.


How is Domestic Waste Formed?

  • Food Waste 🍗 - It includes waste such as vegetables, fruit peel, wasted meats, bones and other foods that have been chucked away.
  • Recyclable Waste ♻️ - It includes waste such as glass, hard plastics, cardboard, paper, and any other waste which can be recycled.
  • General Waste 🗑️ - It includes contaminated waste, used paper dishes and any other usual household rubbish that cannot be recycled.
  • Garden Waste 🌱 - It includes plant materials such as flowers, weeds and grass.
  • Hazardous Waste ⚠️ - It includes batteries, non-edible oils such as car oil and waxes which is considered to be or contains hazardous materials.
