What does “reuse” mean?

Reuse is the practice of using items again—either for their original purpose or a new one—without significant processing, rather than discarding them after a single use. Reusing domestic waste benefits the environment and your wallet by conserving natural resources, reducing landfill pollution, and lowering household expenses.

How can domestic waste be reused?

  • Prevent Landfill Waste: Never throw away an old, unwanted book because paper degradation in tightly packed landfills produces harmful methane gas.
  • Support Public Literacy: Donate surplus reading materials to your local library to expand their public catalog and offer free community resources.
  • Extend Container Lifecycles: Retain empty glass jars, commercial sauce bottles, and durable glass food tubs after cooking.
  • Repurpose Worn Fabrics: Save old, stained window curtains and worn-out bedsheets instead of throwing them into the trash bin.
